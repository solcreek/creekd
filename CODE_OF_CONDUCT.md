# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

This project adopts the [Contributor Covenant version 2.1][cc] as
its code of conduct.

[cc]: https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/

## In short

We commit to making participation in this project a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or
invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Examples of behaviour that contribute to a positive environment:
demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people; being
respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback; accepting
responsibility and apologising to those affected by our mistakes;
focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals but for
the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour: the use of sexualised language
or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind; trolling,
insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political
attacks; public or private harassment; publishing others' private
information, such as a physical or email address, without their
explicit permission; other conduct which could reasonably be
considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub
issues, PRs, discussions, commit messages) and when an individual
is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable
behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers (see
[`MAINTAINERS.md`](MAINTAINERS.md)) via direct email or via
GitHub's private vulnerability reporting (which doubles as a
private channel to maintainers for sensitive matters).

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and
fairly. Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and
security of the reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will follow the Contributor Covenant 2.1 enforcement
ladder:

1. **Correction** — private written warning, clarification of
   the violation, possibly a public statement of context.
2. **Warning** — consequences for continued behaviour, including
   a defined cool-off period during which no public interaction
   with the project is permitted.
3. **Temporary ban** — temporary ban from public interaction;
   violating during the ban escalates to permanent ban.
4. **Permanent ban** — permanent ban from public interaction
   with the project for sustained inappropriate behaviour,
   harassment, or aggression toward individuals or classes of
   individuals.

The full enforcement guidelines text is at the [Contributor
Covenant 2.1 enforcement section][cc-enf].

[cc-enf]: https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines
